Inappropriate Image Detection Using Faster R-CNN

This system uses the Faster R-CNN architecture to detect and filter out NSFW content (images and videos), effectively identifying inappropriate material even in the presence of adversarial attacks. It supports safer content moderation across various platforms.

About This Project

The rapid growth of user-generated content has made automated content moderation essential for maintaining safe online environments. Deep learning models, especially Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), are widely used for detecting harmful or inappropriate content in real time. However, these systems are vulnerable to adversarial attacks, subtle image manipulations that can mislead detection models and allow malicious content to bypass filters.

This research leverages the Faster R-CNN architecture to improve the detection of cloaked or adversarially altered NSFW content. Its two-stage design enables more precise region identification and classification, making it well-suited for identifying subtly distorted inputs. By integrating adversarial purification techniques, the system aims to enhance resilience and reliability in automated content moderation.
